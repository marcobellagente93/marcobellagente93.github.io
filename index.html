Marco Bellagente

PhD Student at University of Heidelberg and MPIK · marco.bellagente@gmail.com

I am in my third year of PhD at Heidelberg university and Max Planck Institute for nuclear physics, spending my time bringing existing deep learning ideas into physics, as well as developing new ML tools.

I have many research and working interests, spanning from theoretical physics to machine learning. Previous research activity included:

  • Normalizing Flows and invertible models;
  • Generative Adversarial Networks for high quality syntetic data generation;
  • Bayesian Neural Networks and bayesian computation;
  • Topology methods for Deep Learning
On top of my research activity, I devote a lot of my free time reading and testing everything I find cool in the ML landscape. This includes, but not limited to:
  • Geometric deep learning;
  • Transformers for NLP and image classification;
I received a Master degree in physics (cum laude) from the University of Milan under the supervision of Stefano Forte with a thesis on high-energy resummation.
